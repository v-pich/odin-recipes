Cambodian Spring Roll

Description

Cambodian spring roll consist of rice paper filled with vegtables and rice noodle.
Some see it as an appetizer but if you eat more then a few it can easily become a healthy meal.
The sauce is a mix of fish sauce, vinegar and water. The sweet and salty semi sour sauce is what ties this dish together.

Ingredients

  • Bonless pork shoulder rib or pork belly
  • Shrimp
  • Instant rice vermicelli noodle
  • Rice paper wrap
  • lettuce
  • Cucumber
  • Lettuce
  • cilantro
  • Thai Basil

    • Dipping Sauce

  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cup vinegar
  • 1 cup fish sauce
  • 2 cups water

Steps

  1. Boil pork until cooked to internal temp of 205℉ When pork is cooked slice it. Layer two to three slices on top of eachother then slice into strips
  2. Peel and deveined shrimp, boil until cooked
  3. Boil noodles for roughly 2 minutes then drain
  4. Rinse all the vegtables and basil set aside
  5. Cut cucumber in half then slice into srips
  6. Seperate the basil leaves from stem, discard stem

Dipping Sauce

combine sugar, vinegar, fish sauce and water into a saucepan, bring to a boil then turn off heat. You can add more sugar or fish sauce, according to your taste

Assemble/Rolling Process

Your going to need a bowl or something wide enough to fit rice paper and a plate that's a little bigger then the rice paper.

  1. Fill the bowl or whatever you may have with warmish hot water, enough to submerge the rica paper
  2. place the rice paper in the water until soft
  3. Remove rice paper and place it flat on the plate
  4. Add vegetables in layers. Place it about 1 inch from the bottom of the rice paper.
  5. Add your noodles
  6. Top it off with pork and shrimp
  7. Roll, starting from the bottom take the inch of rice paper and cover our filling, roll over once, tightly.
  8. Fold in both the sides and tighly roll until your reach the end of the rice paper.
