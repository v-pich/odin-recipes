Memphis Ribs

Description

When it comes to Memphis Ribs, it's all about the dry rub. The rub of pepper and spices, topped off with apple cider vinegar mop sauce is the source of flavor for this rib recipe. Since we are not using BBQ sauce, the rib rely heavily on the dry rub.

Ingredients

  • 1 rack baby back rib

    • Dry Rub

  • 1 tbsp fennel seed
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1/2 tbsp coriander seeds
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp salt
  • 1 tbsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tbsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tbsp onion powder
  • 1/2 tbsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tbsp dried oregano
  • 1/2 tbsp dried mustard
  • 1/2 tsp ground cayenne

    • Mop Sauce

  • 1/2 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/2 tbsp dry rub
  • 1 1/2 tbsp melted butter

Steps

  1. Toast the fennel, coriander and cumin seeds in a pan or skillet, until fragrant. Grind in a spice grinder or use mortar and pestle until in powder form.
  2. In a bowl combine powdered spices with the rest of the dry rub ingredients then set aside.
  3. Whisk together the mop sauce ingredients then set aside
  4. Peel membrane off from the back of the ribs. Apply 3/4 of the dry rub all over bothe sids of the ribs.
  5. Let the rib sit for a minimum of 30 minutes.
  6. Preheat oven to 325℉
  7. place rib bone side down set in an aluminum foil lined baking tray place the tray on the top rack in your oven and bake for 1 1/2 to 2 hours.
  8. Halfway through cover ribs with aluminum foil to protect them from drying.
  9. In the last hour of baking, baste the top of the rib with mop sauce, every 15 min.
  10. Before cutting the rib for serving, sprinkle the rest of the dry rub on top and let rest for 5 min. Slice and serve immediately
